Types of Travel Insurance Coverage Explained

Travel insurance policies bundle various types of coverage. Understanding each component helps you choose the right level of protection for your trip.

1. Emergency Medical Coverage

This is the most critical coverage. It pays for medical treatment you need while traveling due to sudden illness or injury. Coverage includes:

  • Hospital stays and surgery
  • Doctor consultations
  • Prescription medications
  • Diagnostic tests (X-rays, MRIs, blood tests)
  • Emergency dental treatment
  • Ambulance services

Recommended limits: Minimum $100,000 for most destinations. For the USA, Canada, and Japan (where healthcare is extremely expensive), aim for $250,000-$500,000 or higher.

2. Medical Evacuation and Repatriation

If you're seriously ill or injured and need to be transported to a better-equipped hospital or back to Nigeria, this coverage pays for:

  • Air ambulance services
  • Medical escort on commercial flights
  • Ground transportation to appropriate facilities
  • Repatriation of remains in case of death

Why it matters: A medical evacuation flight can cost $50,000-$150,000 depending on distance and medical requirements. This single benefit alone justifies the cost of travel insurance.

3. Trip Cancellation Coverage

Reimburses non-refundable travel costs if you must cancel before departure due to covered reasons such as:

  • Serious illness or injury (you or immediate family)
  • Death in the family
  • Jury duty or legal summons
  • Natural disasters at destination
  • Employer-mandated schedule changes (some policies)
  • Visa denial (some policies)

Tip: "Cancel for any reason" (CFAR) coverage costs more but allows you to cancel for reasons not normally covered, though typically only reimburses 50-75% of costs.

4. Trip Interruption Coverage

If you must cut your trip short and return home early due to a covered emergency, this pays for:

  • Unused, non-refundable portion of your trip
  • Additional transportation costs to return home

5. Baggage and Personal Effects

Covers loss, theft, or damage to your luggage and personal belongings. Also covers essential purchases if your bags are delayed.

  • Baggage loss/theft: Typically $1,000-$3,000 total, with per-item limits of $250-$500
  • Baggage delay: $100-$300 per day for essentials while waiting for delayed luggage

Note: Expensive electronics, jewelry, and cameras often have low sub-limits. Consider additional coverage for valuable items.

6. Travel Delay Coverage

If your flight is significantly delayed (usually 6-12+ hours), this covers reasonable expenses for:

  • Hotel accommodation
  • Meals
  • Local transportation

Typically limited to $150-$300 per day up to a maximum of $500-$1,500.

7. Personal Liability Coverage

Protects you if you accidentally injure someone or damage their property while traveling. Important because liability claims abroad can be substantial.

Schengen Visa Insurance Requirements: Complete Breakdown

If you're applying for a Schengen visa to visit any of the 27 European countries in the Schengen Area, travel insurance is mandatory. The requirements are specific and non-negotiable:

Mandatory Schengen Insurance Requirements

‚úÖ Your Schengen Travel Insurance MUST:

  • Cover all Schengen countries: Your policy must be valid across all 27 member states, not just your destination
  • Minimum ‚Ç¨30,000 medical coverage: This is approximately $33,000 or ‚Ç¶50+ million at current rates
  • Include emergency medical repatriation: Coverage for return to Nigeria if medically necessary
  • Include repatriation of remains: Coverage for return of body in case of death
  • Be valid for entire stay: Coverage must span from entry to exit dates
  • Zero or low deductible preferred: Many embassies prefer policies with no excess for medical claims

Countries in the Schengen Area (2026)

Your insurance must cover travel to any of these countries: Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Croatia, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Italy, Latvia, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, and Switzerland.

Documentation for Visa Application

Your insurance certificate must clearly display:

  • Your full name (matching passport)
  • Coverage dates (matching intended travel dates)
  • Geographic coverage (Schengen Area)
  • Medical coverage amount (minimum ‚Ç¨30,000)
  • Policy number
  • Insurance company name and contact details

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Schengen Insurance Mistakes

  • Wrong dates: Ensure coverage includes your arrival AND departure dates, plus a 1-2 day buffer
  • Single country coverage: Must cover ALL Schengen states, not just your destination
  • Insufficient coverage: ‚Ç¨30,000 is the minimum‚Äîdon't go lower
  • Non-compliant provider: Use recognized insurers; some cheap online policies aren't accepted
  • Missing repatriation: Both medical repatriation and repatriation of remains must be included

Insurance Requirements for Other Destinations

United Kingdom

Travel insurance is not mandatory for UK visa applications, but highly recommended due to NHS charges for visitors. Visitors are charged 150% of NHS rates for treatment. A&E treatment starts free but follow-up care is chargeable.

United States

Not required for visa but ESSENTIAL due to extreme healthcare costs. The US has the world's most expensive healthcare system. A simple emergency room visit can cost $2,000-$5,000. Major treatment runs into hundreds of thousands.

Recommended minimum: $250,000-$500,000 medical coverage for US travel.

Canada

Not mandatory but strongly recommended. Canadian healthcare is expensive for visitors‚Äîhospital stays can exceed CAD $3,000 per day.

United Arab Emirates (Dubai)

Mandatory for Dubai visa applications. Minimum coverage varies by visa type. Dubai has excellent but expensive private healthcare.

Australia

Mandatory health insurance for student visas. Recommended for tourist visas due to high healthcare costs.

How Much Does Travel Insurance Cost in Nigeria?

Travel insurance costs depend on multiple factors. Here's a realistic breakdown of what Nigerians can expect to pay in 2026:

Factors Affecting Price

  • Destination: USA/Canada cost more to insure than Europe or Asia
  • Duration: Longer trips cost more (but per-day rate decreases)
  • Age: Travelers over 60-65 pay higher premiums
  • Coverage level: Higher limits = higher premiums
  • Activities: Adventure sports coverage adds cost
  • Pre-existing conditions: Coverage for these (when available) increases cost

How to Choose the Right Travel Insurance Policy

Follow this step-by-step process to select the optimal policy for your trip:

Step 1: Assess Your Destination Requirements

  • Is insurance mandatory for your visa? (Schengen, Dubai, etc.)
  • How expensive is healthcare at your destination?
  • Are there specific coverage requirements to meet?

Step 2: Calculate Your Trip Value

Add up all non-refundable costs: flights, hotels, tours, activities. This determines how much trip cancellation coverage you need.

Step 3: Consider Your Health and Activities

  • Do you have pre-existing medical conditions?
  • Will you be doing adventure activities (skiing, diving, hiking)?
  • Are you traveling pregnant or with elderly family?

Step 4: Compare Multiple Quotes

Get quotes from at least 3 providers. Compare not just price but:

  • Coverage limits for each category
  • Deductibles/excess amounts
  • Exclusions and limitations
  • Claims process and reputation
  • 24/7 assistance availability

Step 5: Read the Fine Print

Before purchasing, carefully review:

  • What's excluded (often in a separate section)
  • Definition of "pre-existing conditions"
  • Claims notification requirements and timeframes
  • Documentation requirements for claims

How to Make a Successful Travel Insurance Claim

Knowing how to claim properly can mean the difference between receiving payment and rejection. Follow these steps:

Before You Leave Nigeria

  • Save your insurer's emergency hotline number in your phone
  • Carry a printed copy of your policy and emergency contacts
  • Photograph your valuables before packing
  • Keep receipts for expensive items

During a Medical Emergency

  1. Call your insurer immediately (before treatment if possible)
  2. They may direct you to approved hospitals or arrange direct billing
  3. Keep ALL receipts and documentation
  4. Get a medical report before leaving the hospital
  5. Take photos of prescriptions and medications

For Theft or Loss

  1. Report to local police within 24 hours
  2. Obtain a police report (critical for claims)
  3. Report to your insurer immediately
  4. Keep receipts for emergency purchases
  5. Document everything with photos if possible

For Trip Cancellation

  1. Contact your insurer immediately when you know you can't travel
  2. Obtain documentation of the reason (medical certificate, death certificate, etc.)
  3. Keep proof of all non-refundable costs
  4. Document any refunds received

Filing Your Claim

  • Submit claims promptly (most insurers require within 30-90 days)
  • Complete all forms accurately
  • Provide all requested documentation
  • Keep copies of everything you submit
  • Follow up if you don't hear back within 2-3 weeks

Common Exclusions to Watch For

Most travel insurance policies will NOT cover:

  • Pre-existing medical conditions: Conditions you had before buying the policy (definition varies)
  • High-risk activities: Skydiving, bungee jumping, motorsports (unless specifically covered)
  • Travel against medical advice: If a doctor advised you not to travel
  • Travel to high-risk countries: War zones or countries with travel advisories
  • Alcohol or drug-related incidents: Accidents while intoxicated
  • Self-harm or suicide: Mental health emergencies may have limited coverage
  • Pandemics: Some policies exclude pandemic-related claims (check COVID-19 coverage)
  • Valuables left unattended: Items stolen from beaches, cars, or public places
  • Known events: Hurricanes, elections, or events announced before you bought the policy

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I buy travel insurance?

Buy as soon as you book your trip‚Äîideally within 14-21 days of your first booking. Early purchase ensures trip cancellation coverage applies from the moment you book. For visa applications, you'll need the certificate before your embassy appointment. Don't wait until the last minute, as you might forget or face website issues.

Does travel insurance cover COVID-19 in 2026?

Most policies now cover COVID-19 medical treatment abroad just like any other illness. Many also cover trip cancellation if you test positive before departure. However, coverage varies by provider‚Äîalways verify specific COVID-19 coverage before purchasing. Some policies may exclude claims related to government-imposed lockdowns or border closures.

What if I have a pre-existing medical condition?

Standard policies typically exclude pre-existing conditions. However, some insurers offer coverage with medical disclosure and underwriting. You'll usually pay higher premiums. It's crucial to disclose honestly‚Äîif you don't and need to claim for a related condition, your entire claim could be rejected, and you might not receive coverage for unrelated issues either.

Can I extend my travel insurance if I extend my trip?

Many insurers allow policy extensions, but you must request an extension BEFORE your original policy expires. Extensions are not guaranteed‚Äîinsurers may decline if you've had claims or if risks have changed. It's often easier to buy a longer policy initially than to extend later. Contact your insurer as soon as you know your plans are changing.

Is travel insurance worth it for short trips?

Absolutely. Medical emergencies don't care about trip length. A 3-day trip to Dubai can still result in a $50,000 hospital bill if something goes wrong. The cost of travel insurance for short trips is minimal (often ‚Ç¶15,000-25,000), while the potential financial exposure is massive.

What's the difference between travel insurance and health insurance?

Your Nigerian HMO or health insurance typically only covers treatment in Nigeria. Travel insurance specifically covers you abroad and includes travel-specific benefits like trip cancellation, lost baggage, and medical evacuation. You need travel insurance even if you have excellent health coverage in Nigeria.

Can I get a refund if I don't travel?

Most travel insurance policies are non-refundable once purchased. Some insurers offer partial refunds if you cancel before your coverage start date. If you have trip cancellation coverage and cancel for a covered reason, you can claim for your travel costs but not the insurance premium itself.

Final Thoughts: Don't Travel Without Protection

Travel insurance is one of those expenses that feels unnecessary until you need it‚Äîand then it becomes the best money you ever spent. For the relatively small cost of a policy (often less than 2-3% of your total trip cost), you're protecting yourself against potentially life-changing financial consequences.

As a Nigerian traveler, you face unique challenges: healthcare abroad is denominated in dollars or euros, medical evacuation to Nigeria is expensive, and you're far from your support network. Travel insurance is your safety net.

Whether you're traveling for business, pleasure, education, or family visits, make travel insurance a non-negotiable part of your travel planning. Compare policies, understand what you're buying, and travel with confidence knowing you're protected.
