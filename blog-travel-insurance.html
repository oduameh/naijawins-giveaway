Why You Need Travel Insurance

Medical Emergencies

Healthcare abroad is expensive. A hospital stay in the US can cost $10,000+ per day. Surgery or intensive care can run into hundreds of thousands of dollars. Travel insurance covers these costs.

Medical Evacuation

If you need to be flown home or to a better-equipped hospital, medical evacuation can cost $25,000-$100,000+. This is often included in travel insurance.

Trip Cancellation

If you've paid for flights, hotels, and tours but can't travel due to illness, family emergency, or other covered reasons, trip cancellation insurance reimburses your non-refundable costs.

Lost or Delayed Luggage

Airlines sometimes lose luggage. Travel insurance can reimburse you for essential items and the value of lost belongings.

Visa Requirements

Many countries, especially Schengen area nations, require proof of travel insurance for visa approval.

Schengen Visa Insurance Requirements

For Schengen visa applications, your travel insurance must:

  • Cover all Schengen countries
  • Provide minimum ‚Ç¨30,000 medical coverage
  • Include repatriation (return of remains)
  • Be valid for entire duration of stay plus buffer days
  • Have no excess/deductible for medical claims (preferred)

The insurance certificate must clearly state these details for your visa application.

How to Choose the Right Policy

  1. Match coverage to destination: US/Canada need higher medical limits; Schengen has specific requirements
  2. Consider trip value: Ensure trip cancellation covers your non-refundable costs
  3. Check pre-existing conditions: Many policies exclude them
  4. Review exclusions: Adventure sports, certain activities may not be covered
  5. Compare prices: Get quotes from multiple providers
  6. Read reviews: Check claims payment reputation

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I buy travel insurance?

Buy as soon as you book your trip. Some benefits, like trip cancellation, only apply if you purchase before a covered event occurs. For visa applications, you'll need it before your embassy appointment.

Does travel insurance cover COVID-19?

Most policies now cover COVID-19 medical treatment abroad. Some also cover trip cancellation if you test positive before departure. Always verify with your insurer.

What if I have a pre-existing condition?

Standard policies often exclude pre-existing conditions. However, some insurers offer coverage with medical disclosure and possibly higher premiums. Always disclose honestly to avoid claim rejection.

How do I make a claim?

Keep all receipts and documentation. Contact your insurer's hotline immediately for medical emergencies. File claims promptly upon return with completed forms and supporting documents.
