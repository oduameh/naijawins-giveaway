What is a TIN?

The Tax Identification Number (TIN) is a unique 10-digit number assigned to every Nigerian taxpayer. It's used to track all tax obligations and payments. Previously, different tax authorities issued separate numbers, but the Joint Tax Board (JTB) now coordinates a unified TIN system.

When You Need a TIN

  • Filing tax returns
  • Obtaining tax clearance certificates
  • Opening certain corporate bank accounts
  • Bidding for government contracts
  • Property registration and transactions
  • Vehicle registration (in some states)
  • Certain regulatory approvals

How to Register for TIN

For Individuals

  1. Visit the JTB TIN portal (www.tinverification.gov.ng) or FIRS portal
  2. Click "Individual Registration"
  3. Complete the form with personal details, employment information, and address
  4. Upload required documents (ID, passport photo)
  5. Submit and receive your TIN via email

For Companies/Businesses

  1. Visit FIRS portal or JTB TIN portal
  2. Select "Non-Individual Registration"
  3. Provide CAC registration details, business address, and director information
  4. Upload CAC certificate, memorandum, utility bill
  5. Submit and receive company TIN

VAT Registration

If your business supplies taxable goods or services, you must register for VAT with FIRS. After registration, you:

  • Charge 7.5% VAT on your invoices
  • File monthly VAT returns
  • Remit collected VAT to FIRS
  • Claim input VAT on business purchases

VAT Exempt Items

  • Basic food items
  • Medical and pharmaceutical products
  • Educational materials
  • Baby products
  • Agricultural equipment

Filing Tax Returns

Company Income Tax Returns

  • Due within 6 months of financial year-end
  • File through FIRS e-filing portal
  • Include audited financial statements

Personal Income Tax Returns

  • Due by March 31 each year (for previous year)
  • Self-employed must file; employed individuals usually have PAYE deducted

Tax Clearance Certificate (TCC)

A TCC proves you're up-to-date with tax obligations. You need it for:

  • Government contract bidding
  • Bank loan applications
  • Foreign visa applications (sometimes)
  • Registering vehicles
  • Property transactions

Apply through the FIRS office after filing returns and paying taxes due.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is TIN registration free?

Yes, TIN registration is free. Beware of agents who charge excessive fees for this simple process that you can do yourself online.

I'm employed‚Äîdo I need to file taxes?

If you're employed and your employer deducts PAYE, you may not need to file separately. However, if you have multiple income sources, you should file a personal return. Self-employed individuals must file.

What if I haven't been filing taxes?

It's best to regularize your status. FIRS periodically offers amnesty programs. Consult a tax professional to understand your liabilities and options.

What are the penalties for not registering?

Failure to register and file taxes can result in penalties including fines and interest on unpaid taxes. In serious cases, prosecution is possible.
