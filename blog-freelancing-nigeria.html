Getting Paid as a Nigerian Freelancer

  • Payoneer: Widely accepted, USD account, withdraw to Nigerian banks
  • Wise (TransferWise): Low fees, good rates, multiple currencies
  • Domiciliary Account: Receive wire transfers directly
  • PayPal: Limited functionality for Nigeria but usable for some platforms

Building Your Freelance Career

  1. Learn a marketable skill: Focus on one area deeply
  2. Build a portfolio: Create sample projects if needed
  3. Start on platforms: Take smaller projects for reviews
  4. Deliver excellent work: Reviews drive future opportunities
  5. Raise rates gradually: As reviews accumulate, increase prices
  6. Move to direct clients: Eventually, find clients off-platform for better margins

Managing Freelance Income

  • Set aside 20-30% for taxes
  • Build an emergency fund (income can be irregular)
  • Save in both naira and dollars
  • Track all income and expenses
  • Consider health insurance and pension yourself

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to pay tax on freelance income?

Yes. All income is taxable. Register for TIN and file personal income tax. Consider consulting an accountant for proper structuring.

How long until I can earn full-time income?

Varies widely. Some achieve it in 3-6 months; others take a year or more. Consistency, skill level, and niche matter.

Can I freelance while employed?

Yes, many freelancers start as a side hustle. Check your employment contract for any restrictions and manage your time carefully.
