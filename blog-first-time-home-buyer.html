Buying your first home is one of the biggest financial decisions you'll ever make. In Nigeria, where real estate scams are common and property prices are high, first-time buyers need to be especially careful. This guide walks you through every step of the process.

üè° First-Time Buyer Checklist

  • Save at least 20-30% of property price for down payment
  • Get mortgage pre-approval before house hunting
  • Always verify property title documents
  • Conduct physical inspection and engage a surveyor
  • Use a lawyer for all transactions

Step 1: Assess Your Financial Readiness

Calculate What You Can Afford

Your monthly housing cost (mortgage + maintenance) shouldn't exceed 30% of your income. Factor in:

  • Down payment savings
  • Monthly mortgage capacity
  • Closing costs (legal fees, agency fees, taxes)
  • Moving and renovation expenses
  • Emergency fund (keep 3-6 months expenses separate)

Build Your Down Payment

  • High-yield savings accounts
  • Money market funds
  • Treasury bills
  • Automated savings plans

Step 2: Get Mortgage Pre-Approval

Pre-approval shows sellers you're serious and tells you exactly how much you can borrow. See our mortgage guide for details on NHF and bank options.

Step 3: Find the Right Property

Location Factors

  • Proximity to work, schools, hospitals
  • Traffic patterns and commute times
  • Security of the neighborhood
  • Future development plans
  • Flood risk and drainage
  • Access to amenities

Property Considerations

  • Size and layout for your family needs
  • Build quality and age of construction
  • Estate services and maintenance
  • Parking and storage
  • Power and water supply

Step 4: Verify Documentation

This is where many buyers get scammed. Always verify:

  • Title search: Confirm ownership at the lands registry
  • Survey verification: Ensure the property matches the survey plan
  • No encumbrances: Check for existing mortgages or liens
  • Government acquisition: Ensure it's not marked for acquisition
  • Family land issues: Verify authority to sell for family land

Step 5: Make an Offer and Negotiate

Don't pay the asking price. Research comparable sales and negotiate:

  • Price
  • Included fixtures and fittings
  • Timeline for completion
  • Any repairs the seller should make

Step 6: Engage Professionals

  • Property lawyer: Handles title verification, contracts, closing
  • Surveyor: Verifies property boundaries and survey
  • Structural engineer: Assesses building condition (for older properties)

Step 7: Complete the Purchase

  1. Sign sale agreement
  2. Pay deposit (hold in escrow if possible)
  3. Complete mortgage processing
  4. Execute deed of assignment
  5. Pay stamp duty and obtain Governor's consent
  6. Register at lands registry
  7. Receive keys and take possession

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Buying without verifying title
  • Paying cash without receipts or witnesses
  • Not using a lawyer
  • Falling for too-good-to-be-true prices
  • Not inspecting the property thoroughly
  • Ignoring location for cheap price

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the buying process take?

Typically 2-6 months from offer to possession. Mortgage processing, title verification, and government consent can take time.

Is it better to buy or build?

Buying is faster and less stressful. Building can be cheaper but requires significant time, expertise, and oversight. For first-timers, buying is usually safer.

Should I use a real estate agent?

Agents can access more properties and negotiate on your behalf. Just ensure they're reputable and verify everything independently.

Conclusion

Buying your first home is achievable with proper planning, savings discipline, and careful due diligence. Take your time, don't rush into deals, verify everything, and use professionals. Your dream home is waiting.
