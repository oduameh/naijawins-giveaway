Unhappy with your current Pension Fund Administrator (PFA)? Nigerian pension regulations allow you to transfer your Retirement Savings Account (RSA) to another PFA. Whether you're seeking better returns, improved customer service, or more convenient access, this guide explains exactly how to make the switch.

Why Consider Transferring Your PFA?

1. Better Investment Returns

PFAs have different fund management strategies. Some consistently outperform others. Compare historical returns before switching.

2. Poor Customer Service

If you can't get statements, face delays in accessing your account, or experience poor communication, a better PFA may be worthwhile.

3. Convenience

Some PFAs have better digital platforms, more branches, or more responsive customer support channels.

4. Employer Change

When changing jobs, you might want a PFA your new employer works well with (though this isn't required).

How to Transfer Your PFA

Step 1: Choose Your New PFA

Research and select a PENCOM-licensed PFA. Consider:

  • Historical fund performance
  • Customer service reputation
  • Digital platform quality
  • Branch accessibility
  • Fee structure

Step 2: Complete Transfer Request

Visit your new PFA (the receiving PFA) and:

  • Fill out the Transfer Request Form
  • Provide your RSA PIN
  • Submit a copy of valid ID
  • Provide recent passport photograph

Step 3: Wait for Processing

  • New PFA initiates transfer through PENCOM's system
  • Old PFA receives notification
  • Funds are transferred electronically
  • Process typically takes 3-10 working days

Step 4: Confirm Transfer

After transfer:

  • Request statement from new PFA
  • Verify all funds transferred correctly
  • Update your employer with new PFA details

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a fee for transferring PFA?

No. PENCOM regulations prohibit PFAs from charging transfer fees. Both your old and new PFA must process the transfer at no cost to you.

How often can I transfer?

You can transfer once per year. This prevents frequent switching that could disrupt fund management.

Will I lose any contributions during transfer?

No. All your accumulated pension contributions and returns transfer completely. Your RSA PIN remains the same, so contribution tracking is seamless.

Do I need my employer's permission?

No. PFA choice is entirely your decision. However, after transfer, inform your employer/HR to update where future contributions go.
