How to Open a Bank Account in Nigeria: Complete Guide for 2024

Updated December 2024 · 8 min read

Opening a bank account in Nigeria is an essential step toward financial inclusion. Whether you're a student, employee, entrepreneur, or just want a safe place to keep your money, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about opening a bank account in Nigeria.

Types of Bank Accounts in Nigeria

Nigerian banks offer several types of accounts to suit different needs:

1. Savings Account

The most common type of account for individuals. Savings accounts offer modest interest rates on your deposits and are ideal for keeping money you don't need immediate access to. Most banks require a minimum balance of ₦1,000 to ₦5,000.

2. Current Account

Designed for frequent transactions, current accounts are popular with businesses and individuals who need to write cheques regularly. They typically don't earn interest but offer higher transaction limits and cheque books.

3. Fixed Deposit Account

If you want to earn higher interest and don't need access to your money for a set period (usually 30 days to 1 year), fixed deposits are a good option. The longer you lock your money, the higher the interest rate.

4. Domiciliary Account

For those who deal in foreign currencies (USD, GBP, EUR), a domiciliary account allows you to hold and transact in foreign currency without converting to Naira.

Requirements for Opening a Bank Account

The Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) has established tiered KYC (Know Your Customer) requirements. Here's what you need:

Tier 1 Account (Basic)

  • Completed account opening form
  • Passport photograph
  • No ID required
  • Limit: ₦300,000 cumulative balance, ₦50,000 single transaction

Tier 2 Account (Standard)

  • Valid ID (NIN, Voter's Card, Driver's License, or International Passport)
  • Passport photograph
  • Completed account opening form
  • BVN (Bank Verification Number)
  • Limit: ₦500,000 cumulative balance, ₦100,000 single transaction

Tier 3 Account (Full KYC)

  • All Tier 2 requirements
  • Proof of address (utility bill, tenancy agreement)
  • Reference from existing account holder (some banks)
  • Limit: No restrictions

Important: Get Your BVN First

The Bank Verification Number (BVN) is now mandatory for most banking activities in Nigeria. If you don't have one, visit any bank branch with your valid ID to register. The process takes about 10-15 minutes.

Step-by-Step Process to Open an Account

Step 1: Choose Your Bank

Consider factors like:

  • Branch proximity to your home or workplace
  • Quality of mobile/internet banking
  • ATM network coverage
  • Customer service reputation
  • Account maintenance fees

Step 2: Gather Required Documents

Prepare all necessary documents based on the account tier you want. Original documents are usually required for verification, with copies kept by the bank.

Step 3: Visit the Bank or Apply Online

Many banks now offer online account opening. You can start the process on their website or mobile app and complete verification in-branch or via video call.

Step 4: Complete the Application Form

Fill out the account opening form with accurate information. Include details like:

  • Personal information (name, date of birth, address)
  • Employment details
  • Next of kin information
  • BVN

Step 5: Submit and Wait for Verification

The bank will verify your documents and BVN. This usually takes 24-48 hours for most accounts.

Step 6: Fund Your Account and Collect Your Card

Make an initial deposit (if required) and wait for your debit card and account details.

Popular Banks in Nigeria

Here are some of the major banks you can choose from:

  • First Bank of Nigeria: Nigeria's oldest bank with extensive branch network
  • Zenith Bank: Known for strong digital banking services
  • GTBank: Popular for excellent mobile app and customer service
  • Access Bank: Large network after merger with Diamond Bank
  • UBA: Pan-African presence with good mobile banking
  • Kuda Bank: Digital-only bank with no maintenance fees
  • Opay: Mobile money platform with banking features

Digital Banks: A Modern Alternative

Digital banks like Kuda, Opay, PalmPay, and Moniepoint have become increasingly popular in Nigeria. They offer:

  • No account maintenance fees
  • Free transfers (within limits)
  • Quick account opening via mobile app
  • Instant debit card delivery

These are excellent options for young Nigerians who prefer managing their finances entirely from their smartphones.

Tips for Managing Your New Account

  • Download the mobile app: Most transactions can be done from your phone
  • Set up transaction alerts: Get notified of every debit and credit
  • Keep your PIN secure: Never share it with anyone
  • Monitor your account regularly: Check for unauthorized transactions
  • Update your information: Keep your contact details current
