Cost of Living in Lagos Nigeria: Complete 2025 Guide

By NaijaWins Editorial • January 5, 2025 • 14 min read
Advertisement

Lagos is Nigeria's economic hub, attracting millions seeking opportunities. But the city's vibrancy comes with a price—Lagos is consistently ranked as one of the most expensive cities in Africa. Understanding the true cost of living helps you budget realistically and make informed decisions about living and working in Nigeria's commercial capital.

This comprehensive guide breaks down living costs in Lagos across all major expense categories, providing realistic ranges and practical tips for managing your finances in the city.

Housing Costs

Rent by Area

High-end areas like Ikoyi, Victoria Island, and Lekki Phase 1 command premium rents. A one-bedroom apartment costs ₦1.5-4 million annually. Two-bedroom apartments range from ₦2.5-6 million. Three-bedroom units cost ₦4-10 million or more.

Middle-class areas like Gbagada, Magodo, and Surulere offer more moderate pricing. A one-bedroom apartment costs ₦600,000-1.5 million annually. Two-bedroom apartments range from ₦1-2.5 million. Three-bedroom units cost ₦1.5-4 million.

Affordable areas like Ikorodu, Yaba, and parts of the mainland have lower rents. A one-bedroom can be found from ₦300,000-800,000 annually. Two-bedrooms range from ₦500,000-1.5 million. There are cheaper options in outer areas.

Additional Housing Costs

Agency fees are typically 10% of annual rent. Legal fees range from ₦50,000-100,000 or more. A caution deposit equals 1-2 months' rent. Agreement fees cover contract documentation. Service charges in estates can be significant.

Tips for Housing

Location affects daily transport costs—factor this in. Share apartments to reduce costs when starting out. Consider areas near your workplace. Start searching 2-3 months before you need to move. Verify landlord ownership before paying.

Food and Groceries

Monthly Food Costs

Basic groceries for one person cost ₦50,000-100,000 monthly. Cooking at home is significantly cheaper than eating out. Market shopping is cheaper than supermarkets. Bulk buying of staples saves money.

Eating Out

Local restaurants (buka style) cost ₦500-2,000 per meal. Fast food chains charge ₦2,000-5,000 per meal. Mid-range restaurants cost ₦5,000-15,000 per meal. High-end restaurants charge ₦15,000-50,000 or more.

Tips for Food Savings

Cook in bulk and store meals. Buy from local markets like Mile 12 for produce. Limit eating out to special occasions. Plan meals to reduce waste.

Transportation

Public Transport

BRT fares range from ₦200-700 depending on distance. Danfo and keke cost ₦100-500 per trip. Ferries (LAGFERRY) offer water transport options. Monthly public transport for active commuting costs ₦20,000-50,000.

Ride-Hailing

Uber and Bolt trips within Lagos range from ₦1,000-5,000+. Regular use adds up quickly. Surge pricing during peak hours increases costs. Budget ₦50,000-150,000 monthly for regular ride-hailing use.

Private Vehicle

Fuel costs ₦50,000-150,000+ monthly depending on usage. Maintenance costs add significant expense. Car insurance is required. Parking fees apply in commercial areas. Total monthly vehicle costs: ₦100,000-300,000+.

Tips for Transport Savings

Live close to work if possible. Use BRT for regular commutes. Combine trips to reduce travel. Consider carpooling with colleagues.

Utilities

Electricity

DISCO bills vary wildly by area and consumption. Prepaid meters help control spending. Monthly costs range from ₦10,000-50,000+ depending on usage. Generator fuel and maintenance add significant costs. Solar investment increasingly makes sense.

Water

LSWC bills range from ₦5,000-15,000 monthly. Many rely on boreholes or water vendors. Water delivery for tanks is common.

Gas (Cooking)

Refilling a 12.5kg cylinder costs ₦10,000-15,000. Monthly cooking gas costs ₦5,000-15,000 depending on household size.

Internet and Phone

Home internet costs ₦10,000-50,000 monthly depending on speed. Mobile data costs ₦5,000-20,000 monthly. Postpaid plans may offer better value for heavy users.

Healthcare

Medical Costs

Hospital consultations cost ₦5,000-30,000. Medications vary widely by condition. Laboratory tests range from ₦5,000-50,000. Hospitalization without insurance is extremely expensive.

Health Insurance

HMO plans cost ₦50,000-200,000 annually. Coverage varies significantly. Employer-provided insurance is valuable. Budget for out-of-pocket expenses regardless.

Education

Private School Fees

Basic private schools cost ₦100,000-500,000 annually. Mid-range schools cost ₦500,000-1.5 million. Premium private schools cost ₦2-10 million or more. Additional costs include uniforms, books, and extracurriculars.

Public Schools

Public schools are significantly cheaper. Fees are minimal but other costs apply. Quality varies by school and area.

Lifestyle and Entertainment

Entertainment

Cinema tickets cost ₦3,000-8,000. Gym memberships range from ₦20,000-100,000+ monthly. Nightlife can cost ₦10,000-100,000+ per outing. Streaming subscriptions cost ₦2,000-10,000 monthly.

Personal Care

Haircuts and grooming cost ₦2,000-20,000. Salon visits cost ₦5,000-50,000. Personal care products add to monthly expenses.

Sample Monthly Budgets

Basic Budget (Single Person)

Rent (shared or modest): ₦50,000. Food: ₦60,000. Transport: ₦30,000. Utilities: ₦30,000. Phone/Data: ₦10,000. Personal: ₦20,000. Total: approximately ₦200,000 monthly.

Comfortable Budget (Single Person)

Rent (one-bedroom, decent area): ₦100,000. Food: ₦80,000. Transport: ₦50,000. Utilities: ₦50,000. Phone/Data: ₦15,000. Entertainment: ₦30,000. Personal: ₦30,000. Savings: ₦45,000. Total: approximately ₦400,000 monthly.

Family Budget (Couple + 1 Child)

Rent (two-bedroom): ₦150,000. Food: ₦150,000. Transport: ₦80,000. Utilities: ₦80,000. Phone/Data: ₦25,000. School fees (amortized): ₦50,000. Healthcare: ₦20,000. Entertainment: ₦30,000. Personal: ₦50,000. Savings: ₦65,000. Total: approximately ₦700,000 monthly.

Tips for Managing Lagos Costs

Housing

Choose location strategically to minimize transport costs. Consider mainland areas for better value. Negotiate rent—everything is negotiable. Inspect thoroughly before paying.

Daily Living

Cook at home as much as possible. Use public transport where feasible. Track all expenses to identify waste. Build an emergency fund for unexpected costs.

Long-term

Invest in skills that increase earning potential. Explore multiple income streams. Plan for annual rent payments in advance. Consider solar for long-term electricity savings.

Conclusion

Lagos offers tremendous opportunities, but the cost of living requires careful financial planning. Understanding where money goes and making intentional choices about spending are essential for thriving in the city. With proper budgeting and smart choices, you can enjoy what Lagos offers while building financial security.

The key is realistic expectations, diligent tracking, and consistent adjustment of your spending to match your income and goals.
