Types of Business Insurance in Nigeria

1. Property Insurance (Fire and Special Perils)

Protects your business premises, equipment, inventory, and other physical assets from:

  • Fire and lightning damage
  • Explosion
  • Aircraft damage
  • Riot and civil commotion
  • Malicious damage
  • Impact by vehicles
  • Natural disasters (with extensions)

Premium: Typically 0.1% to 0.5% of property value annually

2. Burglary Insurance

Covers loss of property due to theft involving forcible entry or exit. This protects:

  • Stock and inventory
  • Office equipment and furniture
  • Cash (with limits)
  • Damage caused during break-in

3. Public Liability Insurance

Protects against claims from third parties for injury or property damage caused by your business operations. Essential for:

  • Retail stores with customer traffic
  • Manufacturing businesses
  • Service providers working at client premises
  • Event organizers

4. Employers' Liability Insurance

Covers claims from employees who suffer injury or illness due to their work. This is legally required under the Workmen's Compensation Act. Coverage includes:

  • Medical expenses
  • Disability compensation
  • Death benefits
  • Legal defense costs

5. Professional Indemnity Insurance

Protects professionals against claims of negligence, errors, or omissions in their professional services. Critical for:

  • Doctors and healthcare providers
  • Lawyers and legal practitioners
  • Accountants and auditors
  • Architects and engineers
  • IT consultants

6. Product Liability Insurance

Covers claims arising from defective products that cause injury or damage. Important for:

  • Manufacturers
  • Importers and distributors
  • Retailers of consumer products

7. Business Interruption Insurance

Compensates for lost income when your business operations are disrupted by an insured event. Covers:

  • Lost revenue during closure
  • Ongoing fixed costs (rent, salaries)
  • Temporary relocation expenses
  • Extra expenses to maintain operations

8. Money Insurance

Protects cash, cheques, and negotiable instruments while:

  • In transit to/from the bank
  • In your premises during business hours
  • In a safe after hours
  • In employees' custody

9. Goods-in-Transit Insurance

Covers goods while being transported. Essential for:

  • Distribution businesses
  • E-commerce companies
  • Manufacturing companies moving raw materials

How to Choose Business Insurance

Step 1: Assess Your Risks

Identify potential threats to your business:

  • What physical assets need protection?
  • Could customers or employees be injured?
  • What would happen if you couldn't operate for a month?
  • Do you handle cash or valuable inventory?
  • Are there industry-specific risks?

Step 2: Determine Coverage Amounts

Calculate adequate coverage for each risk:

  • Property: Full replacement value of assets
  • Liability: Consider potential claim sizes in your industry
  • Business interruption: 6-12 months of operating expenses

Step 3: Compare Insurance Providers

Get quotes from multiple insurers and compare:

  • Premium costs
  • Coverage limits and exclusions
  • Deductibles (excess amounts)
  • Claims settlement reputation
  • Financial stability of the insurer

Step 4: Consider Package Policies

Many insurers offer bundled business policies that combine several coverage types at a discount. These "shopkeeper" or "office" packages simplify administration and often cost less than separate policies.

The Claims Process

Immediate Steps After an Incident

  1. Ensure safety of all persons
  2. Mitigate further damage where possible
  3. Document the damage (photos, videos)
  4. Report to police if theft or malicious damage
  5. Notify your insurer immediately (usually within 14-30 days)

Filing Your Claim

  • Complete claim forms accurately
  • Provide supporting documentation
  • Cooperate with loss adjusters' inspection
  • Keep records of all communication
  • Follow up regularly on claim status

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underinsurance: Insuring for less than full value means claims are proportionally reduced
  • Not reading policy terms: Understand exclusions before you need to claim
  • Late premium payments: Lapsed coverage leaves you exposed
  • Poor documentation: Keep asset inventories, photos, and receipts
  • Not updating coverage: Review annually as your business grows

Frequently Asked Questions

Is business insurance mandatory in Nigeria?

Some types are mandatory: Employers' Liability (Workmen's Compensation) is required by law. Motor insurance is mandatory for business vehicles. Other types are optional but strongly recommended.

How much does business insurance cost in Nigeria?

Costs vary widely based on business type, size, and coverage. A small retail shop might pay ‚Ç¶50,000-‚Ç¶200,000 annually for basic coverage, while a manufacturing company could pay millions. Get specific quotes for accurate pricing.

Can I get insurance for a home-based business?

Yes, but standard home insurance usually excludes business activities. You need either a home-based business policy or endorsements to your existing home policy.

What is an insurance broker and should I use one?

Insurance brokers are licensed professionals who help you compare policies from multiple insurers. They can be valuable for complex business needs, though you can also buy directly from insurers for simpler requirements.

Conclusion

Business insurance isn't just an expense‚Äîit's protection for everything you've built. A single fire, lawsuit, or theft could wipe out years of hard work without proper coverage.
