Why Save in Dollars?

1. Currency Hedge

The naira has consistently depreciated against major currencies. Saving in dollars protects your purchasing power for international transactions and future needs.

2. International Payments

For school fees abroad, medical tourism, business imports, or international subscriptions, having dollars ready avoids exchange rate shocks.

3. Portfolio Diversification

Spreading your savings across currencies reduces overall risk in your financial portfolio.

4. Long-Term Wealth Preservation

For long-term goals like retirement or children's education, dollar savings maintain value better than naira.

Ways to Save in Dollars in Nigeria

1. Domiciliary Accounts

A domiciliary (domicile) account is a foreign currency account with a Nigerian bank.

  • Currencies: USD, EUR, GBP
  • Minimum deposit: None at most banks
  • Interest: Usually 0% (some banks offer minimal rates)
  • Usage: International transfers, card payments, cash withdrawals

How to open:

  1. Visit your bank with valid ID, passport photograph, and utility bill
  2. Complete the domiciliary account opening form
  3. Some banks allow online opening for existing customers
  4. Fund with cash dollars, international transfers, or naira conversion (at available rates)

3. Foreign Stocks and ETFs

Through investment apps, you can buy shares of US companies like Apple, Google, Amazon, or diversified ETFs like S&P 500 index funds. Your investment is denominated in dollars.

Some banks and fintech platforms offer dollar fixed deposits with interest. Rates are typically 2-5% annually, lower than naira deposits but in a more stable currency.

Things to Consider

Exchange Rate

The rate at which you convert naira to dollars significantly impacts your returns. Compare rates between banks, BDCs (Bureau de Change), and apps before converting.

Fees and Charges

Watch out for:

  • Account maintenance fees
  • Transfer fees
  • Currency conversion spreads
  • Withdrawal charges

Regulatory Considerations

CBN regulations on foreign currency change periodically. Stay informed about current rules regarding dollar transactions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is saving in dollars legal in Nigeria?

Yes. Having a domiciliary account and holding foreign currency is perfectly legal in Nigeria. However, there are regulations around how you can source and use foreign currency that you should follow.

Which bank is best for domiciliary account?

Major banks like GTBank, Access, Zenith, FirstBank, and UBA all offer domiciliary accounts. Compare their dollar card options, transfer fees, and online banking features to choose the best for your needs.

Can I use my domiciliary account for online payments?

Yes, you can get a dollar card linked to your domiciliary account for online payments. Some banks charge annual fees for these cards.

What's better: saving cash dollars or using apps?

Both have merits. Cash dollars are tangible but earn no interest and have theft risk. Apps offer convenience and investment returns but carry platform and regulatory risks. A combination may be wise.
