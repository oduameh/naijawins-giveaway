How to Get Out of Debt in Nigeria: Your Complete Guide to Financial Freedom

Updated December 2024 ¬∑ 15 min read

Are loan app notifications keeping you up at night? Do you owe money to multiple people and feel like you're drowning? Getting out of debt feels impossible when interest keeps piling up‚Äîbut it's not. Thousands of Nigerians escape debt every year, and you can too.

This guide will show you proven debt management strategies that actually work, step by step, so you can break free and start building wealth instead of paying interest.

Step 1: Face Your Debt (No More Hiding)

The first step is always the hardest‚Äîadmitting exactly how much you owe. Many people avoid looking at the total because it's scary. But you can't fix what you don't measure.

Write down ALL your debts:

  • Loan app balances (FairMoney, Carbon, Branch, etc.)
  • Credit card debt
  • Bank loans
  • Money owed to family/friends
  • Cooperative loans
  • Salary advances
  • "Contribution" or ajo you owe

For each debt, note:

  1. Who you owe
  2. Total amount owed
  3. Interest rate
  4. Minimum payment required
  5. Due date

üí° Create Your Debt Inventory

Open a spreadsheet or use a notebook. List every single debt, even the small ones. Add up the total. Yes, it might be shocking‚Äîbut this clarity is powerful. You now know exactly what you're fighting.

Step 2: Stop the Bleeding

Before you can pay off debt, you must stop adding to it.

Immediate Actions:

  • Delete loan apps from your phone (you can reinstall later)
  • Cut up credit cards or freeze them (literally‚Äîin a block of ice)
  • Stop borrowing to pay off other borrowing
  • Avoid "buy now pay later" schemes

‚ö†Ô∏è The Debt Spiral Trap

Taking a new loan to pay an old loan feels like relief, but it's a trap. With interest, you end up owing MORE. This is how people go from ‚Ç¶50,000 debt to ‚Ç¶500,000 debt in months.

Step 3: Create Cash to Attack Debt

You need extra money to pay down debt faster. There are two ways: cut expenses or increase income.

Cut Expenses Ruthlessly:

  • Cook at home instead of eating out
  • Cancel subscriptions you don't need
  • Reduce data plan temporarily
  • Take public transport instead of Uber/Bolt
  • Avoid impulse purchases (wait 24 hours before buying anything)
  • Move to cheaper accommodation if possible

Increase Income:

  • Sell things you don't need (clothes, electronics, furniture)
  • Start a side hustle (see our guide)
  • Work overtime if available
  • Freelance your skills
  • Ask for a raise or find a better-paying job

Every extra Naira goes to debt‚Äînot lifestyle upgrades.

Step 4: Choose Your Debt Payoff Strategy

There are two proven methods to systematically eliminate debt:

Strategy A: Debt Avalanche (Save Most Money)

Pay minimum on all debts, then put ALL extra money toward the highest interest rate debt first.

Example: If you have a loan app at 30% interest and a bank loan at 18%, attack the loan app first.

Pros: Mathematically saves the most money

Cons: Can feel slow if highest interest debt is large

Strategy B: Debt Snowball (Quick Wins)

Pay minimum on all debts, then put ALL extra money toward the smallest balance first.

Example: If you owe ‚Ç¶20,000, ‚Ç¶100,000, and ‚Ç¶300,000, pay off the ‚Ç¶20,000 first.

Pros: Quick wins boost motivation

Cons: May pay more interest overall

Which should you choose? If you're a numbers person, go Avalanche. If you need motivation wins, go Snowball. Both work‚Äîthe best method is the one you'll stick with.

Step 5: Negotiate Your Debts

You may be able to reduce what you owe through negotiation:

For Loan Apps:

  • Contact customer service before you default
  • Ask for a payment plan extension
  • Some apps offer discounts for lump sum payoffs
  • Be honest about your situation

For Bank Loans:

  • Request a restructuring if you're struggling
  • Ask about hardship programs
  • Negotiate lower interest rates (especially if you have a good history)

For Family/Friends:

  • Be honest about your situation
  • Propose a realistic payment schedule
  • Make small, consistent payments to show good faith

Step 6: Build Systems to Stay Debt-Free

Once you're out of debt, stay out:

  1. Build an emergency fund: Start with ‚Ç¶50,000-‚Ç¶100,000 so you don't need loans for emergencies (see our guide)
  2. Create a budget: Know where your money goes each month (see our guide)
  3. Save before you spend: Set up automatic transfers to savings
  4. Use the 24-hour rule: Wait before making non-essential purchases
  5. Keep one loan app for true emergencies only: Don't use it casually

What If You Can't Pay?

If you truly cannot pay your debts, here are your options:

For Loan Apps:

While they can harass you (which is illegal), they cannot:

  • Have you arrested (it's a civil matter, not criminal)
  • Access your bank account without a court order
  • Legally publish your information

Report abusive loan apps to FCCPC (Federal Competition and Consumer Protection Commission).

For Bank Loans:

Banks can take legal action and report to credit bureaus, affecting future borrowing. Try to negotiate first.
