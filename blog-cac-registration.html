Business Structures in Nigeria

1. Business Name (Sole Proprietorship/Enterprise)

The simplest form of business registration for individuals and partnerships.

  • Best for: Small businesses, freelancers, traders
  • Liability: Unlimited (you're personally liable for debts)
  • Cost: ‚Ç¶10,000-‚Ç¶15,000
  • Processing time: 24-48 hours
  • Taxation: Personal income tax rates

2. Limited Liability Company (LLC/LTD)

A separate legal entity from its owners (shareholders).

  • Best for: Growing businesses, those seeking investment, contractors
  • Liability: Limited to share capital
  • Cost: ‚Ç¶20,000-‚Ç¶100,000+ (depends on share capital)
  • Processing time: 3-7 days
  • Taxation: Corporate tax (30% for large companies, lower for small)

3. Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)

Combines partnership flexibility with limited liability.

  • Best for: Professional firms (lawyers, accountants, consultants)
  • Minimum partners: 2

4. Company Limited by Guarantee (CLG)

For non-profit organizations.

  • Best for: NGOs, clubs, associations, charities

How to Register a Business Name Online

Step 1: Create CAC Account

Visit the CAC portal (pre.cac.gov.ng) and create an account with your email and phone number.

Step 2: Check Name Availability

Search for your proposed business name to ensure it's available. You can reserve up to 2 names for ‚Ç¶500.

Step 3: Complete Registration Form

Fill in business details including:

  • Business name
  • Nature of business (select from categories)
  • Business address
  • Proprietor details (name, address, phone, email, BVN)

Step 4: Upload Documents

  • Passport photograph
  • Valid ID (passport, driver's license, NIN slip, or voter's card)
  • Signature

Step 5: Make Payment

Pay the registration fee online via card, bank transfer, or USSD.

Step 6: Receive Certificate

Upon approval (usually 24-48 hours), download your Certificate of Registration.

How to Register a Limited Liability Company

Requirements

  • Minimum 1 shareholder and 1 director
  • Minimum share capital: ‚Ç¶100,000 (for private company)
  • Registered office address in Nigeria
  • At least 1 director must be a Nigerian resident

Documents Needed

  • Passport photographs of all directors/shareholders
  • Valid IDs of all directors/shareholders
  • Signatures of all directors/shareholders
  • Proof of address (utility bill)
  • Consent to act as director forms

Registration Steps

  1. Reserve company name (‚Ç¶500)
  2. Complete incorporation form CAC 1.1
  3. Upload Memorandum and Articles of Association
  4. Submit particulars of directors, shareholders, and secretary
  5. Pay stamping duty (based on share capital) and registration fee
  6. Receive Certificate of Incorporation

After Registration: Next Steps

  • Open a business bank account: Take your CAC certificate to any bank
  • Register for taxes: Get your TIN from FIRS
  • Apply for relevant permits: Industry-specific licenses
  • Annual returns: File yearly with CAC to keep registration active

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I register a business from outside Nigeria?

Yes, you can complete the online registration from anywhere. However, for an LLC, at least one director should be resident in Nigeria, and you need a Nigerian address for the registered office.

How long does CAC registration take?

Business Name registration typically takes 24-48 hours. LLC incorporation takes 3-7 business days. Using an accredited agent may speed up the process.

Should I register a Business Name or LLC?

For small, low-risk businesses, a Business Name is sufficient and cheaper. For businesses seeking credibility, investors, loans, or government contracts, an LLC is better despite higher costs.

Do I need a lawyer to register?

For Business Name registration, you can do it yourself online. For LLC incorporation, while not legally required, using a lawyer or CAC-accredited agent helps ensure proper documentation and faster processing.
