Is Car Insurance Mandatory in Nigeria?

Yes. The Motor Vehicles (Third Party Insurance) Act makes it illegal to drive without insurance. You can be fined, have your vehicle impounded, or face prosecution. FRSC officers regularly check for valid insurance certificates.

Types of Car Insurance in Nigeria

1. Third Party Insurance (Mandatory)

‚Ç¶5,000 - ‚Ç¶15,000/year

The minimum legal requirement. Covers damage or injury you cause to other people and their property‚ÄîNOT your own vehicle.

What it covers:

  • Injury or death of other people
  • Damage to other people's vehicles
  • Damage to other people's property

What it doesn't cover:

  • Your own vehicle
  • Your own injuries
  • Theft of your car
  • Fire damage to your car

2. Third Party, Fire & Theft

‚Ç¶20,000 - ‚Ç¶50,000/year

Everything in Third Party PLUS protection if your car is stolen or damaged by fire.

Additional coverage:

  • Theft of your vehicle
  • Fire damage
  • Attempted theft damage

3. Comprehensive Insurance

3-5% of car value/year

Full coverage for your vehicle and others. The most complete protection available.

What it covers:

  • Everything in Third Party
  • Damage to your own vehicle (accidents)
  • Theft
  • Fire
  • Natural disasters (flood, storm)
  • Vandalism
  • Personal accident cover

How Much Does Car Insurance Cost in Nigeria?

Insurance premiums depend on several factors:

  • Vehicle value: Higher value = higher premium
  • Vehicle age: Older cars may cost more to insure
  • Type of use: Commercial vehicles cost more
  • Location: High-theft areas cost more
  • Driver history: Claims history affects rates

How to Buy Car Insurance

  1. Choose insurance type: Third Party or Comprehensive
  2. Get quotes: Contact multiple companies or use online comparators
  3. Provide documents:
    • Vehicle particulars
    • Driver's license
    • Vehicle license
    • Proof of ownership
  4. Pay premium: Annual payment is most common
  5. Receive certificate: Keep copy in your vehicle

How to Make an Insurance Claim

If you're in an accident or your car is stolen:

  1. Report to police: Get police report within 24 hours
  2. Notify insurer: Call your insurance company immediately
  3. Don't repair: Wait for insurer's assessor to inspect
  4. Gather evidence: Photos, witness contacts, other driver's info
  5. Submit claim form: Fill out all required paperwork
  6. Get assessment: Insurer sends adjuster to evaluate damage
  7. Receive payment: Or repair at approved garage

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Claim Rejection Reasons

  • Driving under influence of alcohol/drugs
  • Unlicensed driver
  • Late reporting (beyond timeframe in policy)
  • False or exaggerated claims
  • Using vehicle for unauthorized purposes

Which Insurance Should You Choose?

Choose Third Party If:

  • Your car is old/low value (under ‚Ç¶2 million)
  • You can afford to repair or replace it yourself
  • You just need to meet legal requirements

Choose Comprehensive If:

  • Your car is new or expensive
  • You have a car loan (usually required by bank)
  • You live in a high-theft or flood-prone area
  • You can't afford to replace your car if damaged/stolen
